Heads up!

A Beer Rating App Case Study

Heads up! allows people to search, rate, and purchase beers in an all-in-one beer app to make an informed decision about their next beer purchase.

Team members: Amy Kim, Justin Mac, Maricela Austgen

Role: UX Researcher, UI Designer, User Testing

Tools Used: Adobe XD, MIRO, Google Tools, Vecteezy, Trello

Time frame: 3 weeks

Project Overview

PROBLEM:

Many beer drinkers are often overwhelmed with the amount of beer options to choose from. On the other hand, some are annoyed when their favorite beer option isn’t available for purchase.

SOLUTION:

Heads up! allows people to search, rate, and purchase beers in an all-in-one beer app to make an informed decision about their next beer purchase.

User Research

We wanted to figure out with user’s would interested in using an app that would help them discover new beers and guide them in their beer-buying decisions. Through user interviews we were able to establish that people typically have a difficult time when choosing a new beer to try and 92.5% said they would find a beer rating app would be useful.

Once we determined user would benefit from this app we created the User Persona, User Insight, Story Board and a Journey Map to better understand the user’s needs.

With a better understanding of the user’s needs and completing a Competitor Analysis we were able map out a User Flow before heading into wireframes and user testing.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

User Testing Key Take-Aways

  • Users wanted to view more information on the home screen.
  • Camera button: large and in the center of the tab bar
  • Users clicked on the stars to view the ratings, rather than the button.
  • Users valued reading reviews vs leaving a review.
  • Improved upon legibility by changing colors.
  • Users didn't feel comfortable with uploading their photo ID. So we decided to dig deeper into other apps and websites that sell alcohol and found we only needed to verify that the user is 21+.

Final Thoughts

Our app improved significantly with each iteration based on the user testing feedback. It was a humbling experience to learn not to get stuck on one design or idea throughout the iteration process as there is always room for improvement!

Working in a group setting helped tremendously from the beginning on getting different opinions and ideas together. We all had different skill sets and were able to come together as a group in helping one another to create this app.
